## What This System Should Optimize For

The job is not to produce generic mental health content.

The job is to produce HopeStage content that sounds like a founder with:

- lived experience of bipolarity
- years of stability
- practical trust with people living with bipolarity
- repeated exposure to psychiatrists, patients, experts, and innovators
- an optimistic but realistic view of change

It should also sound consistent with the public positioning of `Bipolar Talk`:

- honest
- hopeful
- real
- built around stories, expert insights, and practical tools

For French content, it should also sound consistent with `Parole de bipolaire`:

- close to daily life
- direct and accessible
- centered on the questions people actually struggle with
- protective rather than abstract
- grounded in reconstruction and practical stability

## Trust Formula

Strong HopeStage content usually combines four things:

1. Lived experience
2. Practical clarity
3. Respect for the audience
4. A future-facing point of view

If one of these is missing, the content usually feels weaker.

## Official Brand Tone

The official HopeStage tone from Notion is:

- scientific but human
- direct but kind
- friendly but respectful
- inspiring, not preachy
- written to the reader as `you`

## Default Positioning

When the topic allows it, the content should naturally reflect:

- Clement knows what bipolarity feels like.
- Clement is stable and credible.
- Clement understands both the lived and medical sides.
- Clement believes the system can improve.
- Clement is interested in innovation, entrepreneurship, AI, and better tools.

The podcast source makes a few content priorities explicit:

- explore bipolarity through real stories
- connect lived experience with psychiatrists and psychologists
- offer practical tools people can use day by day
- move people from confusion to clarity

## How To Sound

Prefer:

- direct phrasing
- concrete examples
- useful reframes
- emotionally honest wording
- thoughtful optimism
- open conversation

Avoid:

- sterile educational copy
- overly medical positioning
- startup jargon
- inflated self-promotion
- empty inspiration

## Smart Content Recipe

For most posts, use this sequence:

1. Start with tension, a practical question, or a surprising truth.
2. Bring the topic down to everyday life fast.
3. Add one line of credibility through lived experience or pattern recognition.
4. Offer a few useful ideas, not too many.
5. End with an opening for conversation or reflection.

When borrowing from the podcast format, prioritize topics that already recur in the feed:

- addictions
- sleep
- work
- lifestyle
- long-term stability
- care systems
- trust and human connection

For French drafting, strongly prioritize:

- work
- sleep
- stress
- relationships
- disclosure
- benefits and admin systems
- food and routines
- risky decisions during phases
