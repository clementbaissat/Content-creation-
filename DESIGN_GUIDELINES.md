# HopeStage Design Guidelines

This file translates the official HopeStage brand page into a repo-friendly design reference for social assets, prompt generation, landing pages, and future design work.

Source:

- [HopeStage Brand Guidelines (EN)](https://www.notion.so/hopestage/HopeStage-Brand-Guidelines-EN-24239dda6d2880ed93d4e25c0675564d)
- `assets/brand/README.md`

## Visual Direction

The visual language should feel:

- scientific but human
- calm
- human
- credible
- hopeful
- friendly but respectful
- direct without harshness
- precise without becoming cold

The brand represents `scientific hope`: evidence and empathy held together in one system.

## Official Palette

- Elephant Green: `#154545`
- Pale Goldenrod: `#E6E5B3`
- Biloba Flower: `#B7A8EA`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Mint Tulip: `#BCF5EB`
- Dull Orange: `#ED7534`
- Dark: `#252222`

Usage direction from the Notion brand page:

- Elephant Green is the structural anchor for text and core UI.
- Pale Goldenrod works well for large backgrounds.
- Dull Orange is the accent for highlights and calls to action.
- The remaining colors support the secondary system.

## Typography Direction

- Primary font: `Jeko`
- Use a clear hierarchy across digital, print, and product surfaces.
- Typography should communicate calm strength and trust.
- Avoid making the brand feel clinical or generic.

## Logo Meaning

The official logo symbol is described as a rounded lowercase `g` forming a smiling person.

It should communicate:

- stability
- recovery
- humanity
- recognizability

Canonical repo-stored logo and icon files are tracked in:

- `assets/brand/README.md`
- `assets/brand/manifest.json`

## Image Direction

- Use human-centered photography or illustration.
- Prefer natural light.
- Prefer real dignity over dramatic suffering.
- Avoid panic imagery and exaggerated crisis aesthetics.
- Avoid visuals that make people with bipolarity look passive or broken.
- Do not bake copy into images unless the format explicitly requires it.
- Use soft geometry and consistent iconography.

## Composition Rules

- Keep central safe space for multiple crops.
- For Instagram carousel slides, design primarily for `1:1`.
- For single-image feed assets, design for `4:5`, then adapt to `16:9`.
- Favor breathing room over crowded layouts.
- Use contrast to create clarity, not aggression.
- Keep logo lockups, CTA bars, and small metadata away from the outer edges so platform chrome does not crowd them.

## Brand Asset Discipline

- Use canonical logo and icon files from `assets/brand/`.
- Do not pull older HopeStage logo exports from miscellaneous desktop folders when an approved repo asset exists.
- On light backgrounds, prefer `assets/brand/HopeStage logo home.png` or `assets/brand/Frame 10.png`.

## Emoji Rule

- Use neutral or yellow skin-tone emojis when emojis are needed.

## Tone Translation Into Design

If the content says:

- "clarity" -> use space, structure, and calm contrast
- "hope" -> use warmth and light, not hype
- "trust" -> use restraint, simplicity, and consistency
- "innovation" -> use clean systems and strong composition, not sci-fi cliches
