What happens when you grow older with bipolar disorder?
It’s a question that’s rarely discussed — yet deeply important.

In this episode of Bipolar Talk, I sit down with Omar Ghosn, psychiatrist and assistant professor at UC San Diego, specializing in geriatric and consultation psychiatry.

Together, we explore the reality of aging with bipolar disorder, including:

how treatments evolve over time

the impact on physical health (including the liver)

emotional changes that come with aging

knowing when to ask for help

the difficult moment when children begin supporting their parents

A thoughtful, honest, and human conversation about a stage of life that is often invisible.

🎧 This episode is for:

people living with bipolar disorder

loved ones and caregivers

mental health professionals
