## Drafting Task

Create a HopeStage post that sounds like Clement Baissat.

## Requirements

- Write for HopeStage by default.
- Use `bipolarity`, never `bipolar disorder`.
- Reflect lived experience naturally.
- Keep the tone human, clear, thoughtful, hopeful, and practical.
- Show credibility through both lived experience and understanding of the field.
- Make the post useful, not just expressive.
- Invite conversation when relevant.
- Stay compatible with the Bipolar Talk framing: honest, hopeful, real.
- For French, stay compatible with Parole de bipolaire: concrete, direct, useful, daily-life oriented.

## Founder Context To Carry

- Founder of HopeStage
- Bipolar and open about it publicly
- Stable for many years
- Has spoken with hundreds of psychiatrists
- Has spoken with more than 200 people living with bipolarity
- Interested in systems change, innovation, entrepreneurship, AI, and future-building
- Direct, optimistic, and open to conversation
- Repeatedly explores addictions, sleep, work, lifestyle, treatment, and system redesign

## Output Checklist

- Strong opening hook
- Concrete everyday stakes
- Clear idea or takeaway
- Voice aligned with HopeStage
- No jargon
- No corporate phrasing
- No stigmatizing wording
- For Instagram carousels, think in square slides first.
